The Evolution of Artificial Intelligence in Video Games

Discover how AI has transformed games, from the earliest algorithms to modern adaptive systems

What is AI in Games?

AI in games refers to computer algorithms that create responsive, adaptive, or intelligent behaviors in non-playable characters (NPCs) and game environments. Artificial Intelligence in games goes beyond pre-programmed behaviors and linear decision trees that were the norm in early video games.

Modern AI systems can learn from player actions and environmental variables, dynamically changing game difficulty and narrative flow. This creates a more personalized and challenging experience, as AI can adapt to the player's skill level and play style.

AI Pioneers in Games

Pong (1972)

The first example of AI in games. The opponent's "artificial intelligence" was extremely rudimentary but effective: it simply moved its paddle vertically to intercept the ball. Pong's AI simplicity shows how even basic rules can create an engaging challenge.

Pac-Man (1980)

Used Finite State Machines (FSMs) to control the ghosts. Each ghost (Blinky, Pinky, Inky, and Clyde) has a distinct behavior: Blinky chases directly, Pinky tries to ambush, and the others have more complex behaviors. Although simple, FSMs were effective in creating the illusion of intelligence.

Chessmaster (1986)

Represented a leap in AI complexity for games. Unlike Pong or Pac-Man, chess AI needs to plan ahead, evaluate positions, and predict moves. It used algorithms like minimax with alpha-beta pruning, culminating in the historic Deep Blue that defeated Garry Kasparov in 1997.
